Selassie I Prayer

O Lord, Almighty in whom there is no weakness,
Eternal, in whom there is no transience; in admiring your work as well as Your judgements,
A created being even after much searching,
Cannot fathom them-except to a limited extend
It is a subtle secret which a creature, even after
Much exploring, cannot know but which You alone do know; why in the immediate past as well
As now You have Ethiopian people, from ordinary
Man to Emperor, sink in a sea of distress for a time, and why You have made Italian people
Up to its King, to swim in a sea of joy, for a time

Since no creature created in Your image and to Your pattern gives us hope that everything that he begs of You will be done for him until the day You sever his soul from his body,
we Beseech You that Ethiopia should not remain with her freedom extinct and prostrate under an alien ruler,
so the mouth of her people be silenced for fear of foreign governor,
but rather that You will save them by your deeds of Your kindness,
lest they remain in their hearts oppressed through being deprived of their own Ethiopian ruler who was leading them towards civilization under a light yoke with gladness. O Lord, abode of exiles,
light of the blind! Truth and justice are Your thrones.
Receive Us who have been exiled for our freedom Sake,
who have had to leave our country on Account of violent assault.
In praying to You thus, it is not for our righteousness but for Your great mercies
